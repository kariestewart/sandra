About Common Snakes Myths

Hognose snake (Heterodon, Dipsadinae family)

Some facts: Hognose snakes are diurnal and tend to live on a moderatly low humidity level area, as they get their moisture underground rather than above ground. Hognoses are docile but some tend to be spicy. They can even play dead. Most of hognoses grow to 3ft long.

Boa constrictor (Boidae family)

Some facts: Boa constrictors are nocturnal. They also tend to live in a wide varity of enviorments such as wet tropical forests, deserts, open savannas, and even cultivated fields. Females tend to reach to 6.9-9.8ft while males go from 5.9-7.9ft

Kingsnakes (Colubridae family)

Kingsnakes, often mistaken for milksnakes. They can grow to 3-5ft long. Kingsnakes can live in a varity of areas, but more typically spotted with areas near water. They are also diurnal.

Garter snake ( Colubridae family)

Some facts: The garter snakes are one of the few snakes that can be housed together. These snakes typically live near meadows, forests, and gardens. garters snakes are diurnal and grow to 2-3ft long

Corn snakes (Colubridae family)

Corn snakes, commonly also refered to ass red rat snakes are typically found in rocky yet humids areas. These snakes are interesting as the time of day doesn't affect them like nocturals and diurnals, but are actually crepuscular . They typically grow to around 5-6ft long

ball python ( Pythonidae family )

Some facts: These being one of the most common snake pets, are often fine with any enviorment as long as they have atleast a humidity spot and a dry/basking spot (however, all snakes need a humidity box and a basking spot) They grow to 6ft long and tend to be crepuscular.

Burmese Pythons (Pythonidae family)

Burmese snakes are rather large snakes, typically growing up to over 22ft long and are nocturnal. They typically live in more humid areas like swamps. However they have become an invasive species in the everglades in flordia.
