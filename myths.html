About Common Snakes Myths

"Their eye shape determines if they're venomous or not"

This is false. Some species of snakes are venomous have round pupils, their eye shape shows the time of day where they are most active.

"pine needles and milk attract snakes"

This is false. They are not attracted to pine needles, but they may find the pine needles a favorable condition to rest. With milk they couldn't even digest if they even tried.

"Snakes skin are slimy."

This is false. Snake skin is not slimy, it feels more like an old wallet or a smooth basketball. as snakes tend to be more on the dry side.

"Snakes are deaf."

This is false. While snakes don't have outer ears, they have inner ears reffered to as the columella. Though their hearing isn't as good as their sense of taste.

"Snakes are poisonous."

This is false. Snakes are venomous. Venom is injected while poison is consumed.

"Snakes size up prey"

This is obsured. Snakes are not that bright as an animal, they are opportunisitc animals and if they even tried they'll realize that you just won't fit.

"Snakes have no bones."

This is false. Snakes are vertebrates. They have a vertebal column down their back and have 200-400 vertebrae

"It hurts to help a snake shed its skin"

This is only true if the snake hasn't already started to shed. But it doesn't hurt to gently assist after its already started to shed. As its required to help snakes shed during certain situations.
